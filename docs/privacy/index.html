AppExpose — Local HTTP/API Server

This Privacy Policy describes how Expose ("the App", "we", "us") handles your information when you use the App on iOS or iPadOS. We have designed the App to operate locally on your device and to keep your data on your device. This document explains, in plain terms, what that means and the few exceptions you should be aware of.

1. Summary

  • The App runs an HTTP server on your device, on your local network only. It does not expose your device to the public internet.
  • We do not collect, transmit, or sell the contents of your routes, files, folders, photos, videos, local request logs, or access tokens.
  • The production App includes limited analytics, crash diagnostics, and purchase infrastructure through Firebase, RevenueCat, and Apple platform services. Those systems are used for app operations and commerce, not for collecting the content you expose through the local server.
  • Access tokens generated by the App exist for the current running server session and are never sent to Bonjour, LLC.
  • The App accesses photos, files, and folders only when you explicitly pick them, and only within the scope you grant.
  • You can revoke any permission at any time in iOS Settings, and you can remove all data created by the App by uninstalling it.

2. Information We Do Not Receive From Local-Server Features

Through Expose's local-server features, Bonjour, LLC does not receive any of the following:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or other contact details (we do not require an account).
  • The contents of files, folders, photos, videos, or HTTP responses you expose through the App.
  • Local-client IP addresses captured in request logs. Those logs can remain on-device for the session workflow, but they are never transmitted to us.
  • Device identifiers or advertising identifiers from clients on the local network.
  • The contents of route bodies, files, folders, photos, videos, local HTTP request logs, access tokens, local route URLs, and local-client IP addresses are not sent to Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, or RevenueCat.
  • Location data. The App does not request location permission, does not read GPS, and does not derive location from photos unless you explicitly enable EXIF/location metadata for a Photo Source.

3. Information Stored on Your Device

The following information is created and stored only on your device. It never leaves your device unless you choose to share it (for example, by sending an exported log file to a teammate).

  • Server configuration: routes, route paths, header rules, and source references you create in the App.
  • Access tokens: randomly generated 24-character bearer tokens are generated when the server starts and kept for the current session so the App can gate access to your local server. They are not synced and are not persisted as reusable account credentials.
  • Request logs: each session keeps a rolling log of incoming HTTP requests (method, path, status code, duration, bytes, remote address, matched route). Tokens are removed from the log before storage.
  • Cached files: thumbnails for the Photo Picker, temporary downloads pulled from File Provider / iCloud, and large response temp files used to keep memory low. You can clear all caches from Settings → Cache.
  • Preferences: appearance, language, default port.

4. Information You Knowingly Share

  • HTTP responses on your local network. The whole point of the App is to serve content to other devices on the same Wi-Fi. When you start the server and share a URL or QR code, anyone with the URL and the current access token (when token protection is enabled) can read the content of the routes you have configured. We have no visibility into these requests.
  • Email and feedback. When you use "Contact Developer" to write to support@bonjour.dev, your email address, message contents, and the diagnostic details prefilled into the email body (app version, build commit, bundle identifier, device model, system version, and locale) are visible to us so that we can reply. You can edit or remove these details before sending. We use this information solely to respond to your message and do not share it with third parties.
  • App Store review. When you tap "Rate on App Store", your review is governed by Apple's privacy practices, not ours.

6. Network Behavior

  • The local server listens on a TCP port you configure (default: 8080) and binds to the local network only. It does not open ports on your router and does not use a Bonjour-operated relay to serve your content.
  • Separately from the local server, the App may make outbound requests needed for App Store purchases, RevenueCat entitlement processing, Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, App Store review prompts, or links you tap in Settings.
  • The App does not include public relays, tunneling, mDNS / Bonjour discovery, HTTPS termination via a remote service, or WebDAV.
  • When you contact support, your email is sent through Apple's Mail app using your own email account.
  • When you tap "Rate on App Store", iOS opens the App Store via Apple APIs.

7. Children

Expose is a developer tool not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us through the App, please contact us at support@bonjour.dev and we will delete it.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

  • All on-device data persists until you remove it. You can:
  • Clear caches in Settings → Cache.
  • Delete request logs (per-session or all sessions) from the Logs page.
  • Delete sources, routes, and saved configuration from their respective pages.
  • Uninstall the App, which removes App-managed on-device configuration, caches, and logs.
  • Because data is on-device, deletion is immediate and not reversible.
  • Uninstalling the App does not delete App Store transaction records managed by Apple, entitlement or purchase records processed by RevenueCat, or analytics/crash records that were already transmitted before uninstall.

9. Security

  • Random access tokens are generated locally with a cryptographically secure random source.
  • Tokens are stripped from URL query strings before requests are written to the log file.
  • The server defaults to read-only operations. The first version of the App does not accept uploads, writes, deletes, or PUT JSON payloads.
  • DNS rebinding protections (Host validation, Origin checks, default-deny CORS) are enabled by default.
  • We do not control the security of the local Wi-Fi network you connect to. You should not run the App on untrusted networks unless you understand the implications.

10. Third-Party Services

The production App currently uses the following external services:

  • Apple App Store / StoreKit: processes purchases, subscriptions, refunds, review prompts, and App Store account-level commerce flows.
  • RevenueCat: processes Apple purchase receipts and entitlement state so the App can unlock paid plans, restore purchases, and reflect subscription updates.
  • Firebase Analytics: records limited product telemetry such as app launch events and one-time app transaction metadata used to understand install / purchase context. The App does not intentionally send route contents, file names, photo identifiers, local request URLs, or access tokens as analytics parameters.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: records crash and non-fatal diagnostic information used to improve stability. Diagnostic context must not include route bodies, access tokens, or the contents of files/photos served through the local server.

These services have their own privacy practices and security controls. We expect any service handling App data to provide protections consistent with this policy and the limited purposes described above.

11. International Users

Your routes, files, photos, videos, and local request logs remain on your device. Operational data handled by Apple, RevenueCat, or Firebase may be processed in the regions where those providers operate their services.

12. Your Rights

We do not maintain an Expose user account or remotely store the contents you serve through the local server. For support email you send us directly, you may request deletion or correction by replying to our support address. For purchase, analytics, or crash information handled by Apple, RevenueCat, or Firebase, requests are handled through the controls and processes those providers make available, subject to applicable law.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when laws change, when the App's behavior changes, or when we add or remove features. The "Last updated" date at the top of this document indicates the latest revision. If a change is material — for example, if the App ever begins to collect data remotely — we will surface the change in-app before the new behavior takes effect.
